# Agent Payment Standard (APS) Project Governance

To maintain the neutrality and integrity of the Agent Payment Standard (APS), we operate under a transparent, meritocratic governance model.

## 1. Governance Structure

### The Technical Steering Committee (TSC)
The TSC is the primary decision-making body for the technical specification. 
- **Responsibility:** Reviewing RFCs, approving major version releases, and ensuring alignment with Core Principles.
- **Current Composition:** [Your Name/Handle] (Acting Chair), [Invite 1-2 advisors or leave as 'Open for Nomination'].

### The Community
Anyone can join the community by contributing to the GitHub repository, participating in discussions, or proposing RFCs.

## 2. Decision Making: The RFC Process
All changes to the APS must follow the **Request for Comments (RFC)** process to ensure neutrality and peer review:
1. **Proposal:** Submit an Issue or a Draft PR labeled `RFC`.
2. **Discussion:** A mandatory 14-day public comment period.
3. **Consensus:** Decisions are made by "Lazy Consensus" (if no major objections are raised) or a simple majority vote by the TSC.

## 3. Neutrality & Intellectual Property
- **No Vendor Lock-in:** No proposal shall be accepted if it creates an unfair advantage for a specific commercial entity.
- **Licensing:** All contributions are made under the **Apache License 2.0**. By contributing, you agree that your input is royalty-free and can be used globally without restriction.

## 4. Conflict of Interest
TSC members must disclose any affiliations with commercial payment providers or AI labs. In cases of direct conflict, the member shall recuse themselves from the final vote on related specifications.
